About

The Dallas Cowboys, founded in 1960, stand as one of the most iconic and successful franchises in the NFL. With a rich history and a fan base that spans the globe, the Cowboys have earned the nickname "America's Team." The team plays its home games at the AT&T Stadium in Arlington, Texas. The Cowboys have seen numerous triumphs, including five Super Bowl victories in the 1970s. The team has also produced legendary players like Emmitt Smith, Troy Aikman, and Michael Irvin. Despite facing challenges in recent seasons, the Cowboys continue to be a dominant force in the NFL. They are currently the most valuable sports team in the world. The franchise's enduring legacy and massive fan following solidifies its place in football history.

Dak Prescott, born on July 29, 1993, is an accomplished American football quarterback who plays for the Dallas Cowboys in the NFL. Emmit Smith

Emmitt Smith is a retired American football running back widely regarded as one of the greatest players in NFL history. Smith played the majority of his illustrious career with the Dallas Cowboys and holds the all-time rushing record in the league, amassing 18,355 yards over 15 seasons.
